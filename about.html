HISTORY

The municipality of Pamplona is rich not only of agricultural products but of natural wonders as well. The area of what we know today as Pamplona was just another barrio from Tanjay City. It was not until 1950 when the municipality was created under the Republic Act No 535 on the 16th of June. Most of the land area of Pamplona is covered with valleys, hills and plantations. Today Pamplona stands tall with 16 barangays.

WEATHER IN PAMPLONA

Pamplona has an average rainfall of between 12 and 170 millimeters of rain with October being the rainiest and April being the driest month. The temperature can range from 24 degrees centigrade for the cooler months to around 34 degrees centigrade for the hot months.


ECONOMY IN PAMPLONA

The municipality is mainly an agricultural area with the main cash food crops being coconuts, sugar canes, coffee and bananas. Other crops which are grown in this area include rice and corn, however this is only grown in small scale terms by some farmers. Farmers also keep cows, sheep's, goats and chicken to supplement the agricultural activities.
